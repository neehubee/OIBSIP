Nikola Tesla

The brilliant inventor who revolutionized electricity and paved the way for modern technology.

Here's a timeline of Tesla's life:

  • 1856 - Born in Smiljan, Austrian Empire (modern-day Croatia)
  • 1875 - Enrolls at Austrian Polytechnic in Graz to study electrical engineering
  • 1884 - Moves to the United States and begins working with Thomas Edison
  • 1887 - Develops the induction motor and polyphase alternating current (AC) system
  • 1891 - Invents the Tesla coil, a high-voltage transformer circuit
  • 1893 - Demonstrates wireless power transmission and AC lighting at the World's Fair
  • 1895 - Builds one of the first hydroelectric power plants at Niagara Falls
  • 1901 - Begins construction of the Wardenclyffe Tower for wireless communication
  • 1917 - Wardenclyffe Tower is demolished due to financial issues
  • 1931 - Featured on the cover of Time magazine on his 75th birthday
  • 1943 - Dies in New York City at the age of 86
